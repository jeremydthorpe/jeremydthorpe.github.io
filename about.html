More about me...

I’ve always been a naturally curious person. I like learning about different people, perspectives, and how things work. That shows up in my work too. I’m always picking up new instructional design tools, taking courses, or reading, whether it’s history or science fiction.

I grew up in Utah and spent a lot of time in the mountains. Summers were mostly hiking and camping, and in the winter I was skiing as much as I could. Being out there taught me a lot without really realizing it at the time. You learn how to adjust, figure things out, and keep going when things don’t go as planned. That mindset has stuck with me and definitely shows up in how I approach problems at work.

I like hearing different perspectives and taking the time to understand a problem before jumping to a solution. I’ve worked with people from all over, as well as teams close to home, and I’ve learned that the best outcomes usually come from collaboration and being open to feedback.

As an instructional designer, I focus on making learning practical and relevant. I spend time understanding who the learners are, what they need, and what’s going to actually help them do their job better. My goal is always to create learning that people can actually use, not just something they have to complete.
